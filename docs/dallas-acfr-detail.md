## KEY RISK FLAGS

1. **DPFP Combined Plan severely underfunded at 34.05% (AVA) / 36.19% (MVA)** - one of the worst-funded large public pension plans in the U.S. UAAL of $3.7 billion.

2. **Funding Agreement contribution ($223.7M) is LESS than the ADC ($257.2M)** - Segal warns "the Normal Cost component of the Funding Agreement contribution is less than the current employer Normal Cost... increasing reliance on future contribution increases or favorable experience."

3. **ERF funded ratio declined from 70.1% to 66.9%** despite 9.14% investment return, driven by unfavorable 2022 losses being fully recognized, COLAs exceeding assumptions, and salary increases above expectations. UAAL grew from $1.64B to $1.86B.

4. **ERF contribution deficiency** - City contributed $79.8M vs. ADC of $125.0M for FY2024 (64% of ADC). New Chapter 40A changes approved by voters in Nov 2024 will phase in full ADC over 5 years.

5. **Total unrestricted net position deficit of $4.73 billion** across all government activities, dominated by pension and OPEB liabilities.

6. **Benefits exceed contributions at both DPFP and ERF** - investment returns must bridge the gap. DPFP paid $345M in benefits vs. $261M in contributions (2024). ERF paid $349M vs. $156M in contributions.
