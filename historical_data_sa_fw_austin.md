## NOTES AND GAPS

### Data Successfully Extracted:
- **San Antonio**: All 10 categories for FY2015-2024. Pension data (TMRS + Fire & Police), assessed values, tax rates, population, debt, revenue sources (Continuing Disclosure Table 18), economic data all from ACFR and Continuing Disclosure.
- **Fort Worth**: All 10 categories for FY2016-2025 (FW ACFR is FY2025). Pension (FWERS), fund balances, revenues by source, assessed values, tax rates (with Operating/Debt Service breakdown), population, total/net debt per capita, median family income, per capita income, and unemployment -- all from FW ACFR Statistical Section Tables 2-26.
- **Austin**: All 10 categories for FY2015-2024 (verified against ACFR Statistical Section Tables 1-17). RSI pension data (3 plans: COAERS, Police, Fire). Fund balances (Table 4), revenues/expenditures (Table 5), tax revenues by source (Table 6), assessed values and tax rates (Tables 7-8), principal taxpayers (Table 10), outstanding debt (Table 12), net bonded debt (Table 13), legal debt margin (Table 15), pledged revenue coverage (Table 16), and demographic/economic statistics (Table 17).

### Known Gaps:
1. **SA General Fund Balance FY2015-2019**: The Continuing Disclosure Table 16 only covers FY2020-2024. Earlier years would require older ACFRs or disclosure documents.
2. **Median Household Income**: SA ACFR reports Per Capita Personal Income (not MHI). FW ACFR reports Median Family Income (typically higher than MHI). Austin reports MSA-level MHI. True city-level MHI requires Census ACS 5-year estimates.
3. **FW Note on Fiscal Year**: Fort Worth ACFR covers FY2025 (ending 9/30/2025), so its 10-year range is FY2016-2025, one year ahead of SA (FY2024 ACFR) and Austin (FY2024 ACFR). Comparisons should note this offset.
4. **Austin COAERS**: The funded ratio declined significantly from 70.85% (2021) to 50.30% (2022), largely due to investment losses and a blended discount rate change. Police Officers' plan dropped to 37.72% in 2018 due to assumption changes.
5. **Austin Reserve Ratios**: Austin's General Fund includes 12 sub-funds. The unassigned balance is a conservative measure; the Budget Stabilization Reserve ($161.6M in FY2024) provides additional cushion not reflected in the "unassigned" line.
6. **FW Net Bonded Debt Per Capita**: Table 14 reports per capita in $000s format (e.g., $0.948 = $948 per capita). The Total Primary Government debt per capita from Table 13 is in whole dollars.
