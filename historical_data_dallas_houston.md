## DATA GAPS & NOTES

1. **Dallas FY2015-2016 pension funded ratios**: The RSI schedule notes that 2015-2016 used different reporting bases, so exact funded ratios for those years are approximate or unavailable on the same basis.

2. **Houston does not report "Median Household Income" directly** in its ACFR demographic table. It reports Per Capita Income for the Houston-Pasadena-The Woodlands MSA. FY2024-2025 per capita income data was "not available" at time of publication.

3. **Houston FY2016 is the earliest year** in the 10-year statistical tables (Houston FY ends June 30, so FY2016 = July 2015 - June 2016). Houston's tables do not go back to FY2015 in this ACFR.

4. **Dallas fiscal year** ends September 30; **Houston fiscal year** ends June 30. Comparisons should note this ~3-month offset.

5. **Dallas pension plans**: ERF = Employees' Retirement Fund (civilian); Combined Plan = Dallas Police & Fire Pension System combined plan; Supplemental Plan = DPFPS supplemental plan. As of FY2024, City Council approved pension reforms for both ERF and DPFPS with 5-year phase-in contributions beginning FY2025.

6. **Houston pension plans**: HFRRF = Houston Firefighters' Relief and Retirement Fund; HMEPS = Houston Municipal Employees Pension System; HPOPS = Houston Police Officers' Pension System. The 2017 pension reform (SB 2190) restructured all three plans. HPOPS FY2018 shows an anomalous $887M contribution (one-time pension obligation bond proceeds).

7. **Houston general fund expenditures** in FY2018 and FY2025 include elevated figures due to one-time debt issuance proceeds flowing through as expenditures. The "noncapital expenditures" metric may be more appropriate for reserve ratio calculations.

8. **Census QuickFacts** data fetch timed out. Key reference values from ACS 2023 5-year estimates: Dallas MHI ~$56,588; Houston MHI ~$56,019.
